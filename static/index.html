Equity Curve

The equity curve shows your portfolio value over time, starting from the initial capital at the left edge of the selected date range. Each data point reflects the account balance after any trade that closed on that candle.

Green fill means the portfolio is above its starting value — you're in profit. Red fill means it has dropped below — you're in a loss relative to where you started in this window.

Red shaded regions are drawdown periods: stretches where equity fell from a previous peak and had not yet recovered. The depth and duration of these bands show how painful the strategy can be to hold through.

Use scroll to zoom and click-drag to pan the view. The equity chart and the price chart below share the same x-axis zoom level and a synced crosshair — hover either chart to see the matching moment on both.

Dashed baselineStarting equity for this window — the break-even line.
Flat sectionsPeriods with no open or closed trades — cash is idle.
Sharp steps up/downA trade just closed at take-profit or stop-loss.
Price & Trades

The candlestick chart shows raw OHLC price action for the selected symbol. Each candle covers one 15-minute interval. Green candles closed higher than they opened; red candles closed lower.

Trade signals from the strategy are overlaid directly on the price:

▲ Green triangleStrategy entered a long position at this price and time.
▼ Red triangleStrategy exited a position (stop-loss, take-profit, or opposite signal).

The chart starts zoomed to the most recent 200 candles. Scroll to zoom in or out, and click-drag to pan through history. Zoom is synced with the equity curve above.

Clicking a row in the Trade Log below will pan this chart to centre on that trade and highlight its entry and exit markers with a white border.

▼  What do these numbers mean? ▼
Win RatePercentage of closed trades that ended in profit. A strategy can be profitable with a win rate below 50% if winners are significantly larger than losers.
Avg WinMean dollar profit across all winning trades. Compare this to Avg Loss to understand the reward side of the risk-reward ratio.
Avg LossMean dollar loss across all losing trades. This strategy targets 1× ATR as the stop distance, so Avg Loss reflects how volatile the asset was at entry.
Profit FactorTotal gross profit divided by total gross loss. A value above 1.0 means the strategy made more than it lost overall. Above 1.5 is generally considered solid; above 2.0 is strong.
Max DrawdownThe largest peak-to-trough decline in portfolio value within the selected window, expressed as a percentage. This is the worst-case loss a trader would have experienced holding the strategy.
Sharpe RatioAnnualised return per unit of volatility, calculated from per-candle equity returns (365 × 96 periods/year for 15 m crypto). Above 1.0 is acceptable, above 2.0 is excellent. Does not account for trading fees or slippage.
